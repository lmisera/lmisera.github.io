Lucas Misera

Policy analyst | Data analyst | Entry-level developer

Lucas Misera is a policy analyst from Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania. In 2018, Lucas graduated from Kent State University with degrees in economics and finance. In 2020, he graduated from the University of Michigan with a master's degree in public policy.

Currently, Lucas works for the Federal Reserve Bank of Cleveland in their Community Development Department. His work centers around small businesses, specifically their access to financing. Since June 2020, he's led the programming, cleaning, and analysis of the Small Business Credit Survey, a national survey of over 15,000 businesses across the U.S.

Prior to joining the Cleveland Fed, Lucas worked as a research assistant for the University of Michigan's Center on Finance, Law, and Policy, primarily supporting the Detroit Neighborhood Entrepreneur's Project. At Kent State, Lucas was an entrepreneurial reporting intern for 89.7 WKSU, covering small-business issues in Northeast Ohio.

Federal Reserve Bank of Cleveland| Cleveland, OH | May 2019 - Present

Description Following a three-month internship in 2019, Lucas joined the Federal Reserve Bank of Cleveland's Community Development as a full-time CD analyst in June 2020. Playing a pivotal role in transferring leadership of the Small Business Credit Survey (SBCS) from the New York Fed to Cleveland, he was promoted in early 2021 to policy analyst. Lucas continues to play a key role in programming, cleaning, and analyzing the SBCS.

Highlights

Since joining the Cleveland Fed, Lucas has led the successful transition of the SBCS's leadership from New York to Cleveland. Having sucessfully led two full annual survey cycles, the impact of the survey during his time with the team has been substantial. The SBCS has been cited by a wide range of media outlets, like Bloomberg, Forbes, and American Banker. The survey has also been cited by key policymakers, like Congresswoman Velazquez of the Committee on Small Business or Chairman Jerome Powell of the Federal Reserve.

Lucas also has experience presenting SBCS findings to Federal Reserve Bank of Cleveland leadership, external researchers, and media outlets. In 2022, he presented at the National Community Reinvestment Coalition's 2022 Just Economy Conference in Washington, D.C., as well as to members of the National Association of Business Economists.
